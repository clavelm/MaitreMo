Nothing’s ever fair.

Par Maître Mô Histoires vraies Le 31 octobre 2011, à 7 h 44. Lu 90 065 fois. Le 25 novembre 2011  inceste, infanticide, plainte, prescription 233 commentaires

[Warning : this story can be hard]

Barbara “pushes” for the last time, with all her strength, her face is red, she holds Xavier’s hand as she wants to break it, she dimly hears him talking, among the heartening of the midwife, “There my dear, there, she is here, I can see her head, she is here, you’re doing well my dear…”

She was in pain, first contractions; then they placed the epidural, quite fast and successfully, and it calmed down, until ten minutes ago, where she felt that this time, that was it : she was going to give birth to their daughter! She breathed, pushed, stopped, did everything right, as she had learned during the preparation for childbirth sessions – she applied herself to do so, studious and impatient, as they have been waiting for it for, how long? Six years? No way to blow it, they did everything for it to go the best way, Barbara, even though she was only thirty-two, had been under special medical care, everything was going to be fine : they wanted her so much, this soon to be born little daughter.

“She is there, Barbara, you’re doing good. Stop pushing, completely, OK? Stop, stop, I’m taking her shoulder out, it’s alright, there it is…” The midwives are adorable and capable, they’re reassuring her , she is confident. She “stops”, but anyway she doesn’t feel a thing, at this very moment; pictures flash in front of her closed eyes, while she releases her breath slowly, in small blows between her half-closed lips, the way she was taught, and she feels like smiling : the first tests, when they thought they couldn’t keep trying to have a child, and there might be a problem, their common relief when they learned that no, no problem, they just had to be patient; flash on the bottle of champagne Xavier had put in the trunk of the Audi, near the small pregnancy suitcase, he will fetch them both in a moment; flash on the dazed and fond smile of Xavier a few weeks earlier, when the little Camille – they will name her Camille, they agreed on that immediately – started pushing from the inside with her hands and feet, causing moving bumps on her belly, like moving dunes, Xavier exclaiming, amazed “It’s not real, it’s Alien!”…

“There it is, it’s great; you are going to push one more time, OK? It’s almost over. Come on, here we go, push push push… She is coming out, wonderful, very good”.  She barely makes an effort, this time, she felt the hardest part was behind, and she feels her baby coming out of her, confusedly. She is frankly smiling, now, because she heard one of the midwives ask Xavier if he wanted to “finish getting her out”, and he said no, her sweetheart, with a small pale voice, “I’m afraid I will break her”, half laughing, half crying, she loves her; she opens her eyes, and see the beautiful face of Xavier, moved to the point he is almost crying, leaning over her, and above all Camille, this small alien, who is bawling, and which she is holding in front of her, now, while her feet are still inside her, and she is asked to bring slowly on her belly. She does so, cries, her baby is lying on her, all warm, she doesn’t weigh anything, she is not very dirty, in the end, she laughs, “Very good, there, you have a daughter”.

Barbara looks at her, she is still smiling, this little wrinkled, red face, this tiny body giggling a the end of the cord, lying on her too, my God, how did this thing fit in my womb? Emotions are overwhelming her, she feels like she has been drugged.

Xavier kisses her, she can feel his tears on her cheek, he whispers to her that she is beautiful.

They put some king of surgical pliers on the umbilical cord, near her bellybutton, folded it in a compress. A midwife suddenly, in the eyesight of Barbara, holds big scissors to Xavier : “Do you want to cut the cord, Sir?” These scissors are huge. Scissors like there was at…

“Get in your room !” It came out of nowhere, in her head. What? What is it doing here, this order she hasn’t heard for years? She never thought about it, she had swept it all away, buried everything, not right now, she wants to keep these pictures away, not now, it not possible, everything is coming back, everything is coming back, no! She gets tensed up from head to toe, her whole body, she has the impression that they switched out every lights, there is no light any more; yet, while an absolute panic, freezing, staggering, gets her, she sees Xavier, smiling and hesitating, a halo of light around him, as before, as always, as the light of the bare lamp hanging from her room ceiling, there, in Hell… Xavier makes an unsteady move, she feels some kind of cramp stiffening her body, she is frozen, can’t he see ? He smiles, and moves his huge hands toward the scissors, “I don’t know if… You show me, right?”, with his shy smile; Barbara watches this hand over Camille, who the midwives rolled over, she sees her girl’s genitals, big and all red, like a monkey’s, she sees this fucking hairy hand, the scissors, the other hand which is going to, oh my God, which is going to touch this little girl! The wave, this huge wave of fear and pain she had forgotten, she thought she would never see again rushes to her at full speed… It came back, it’s here, it’s drowning her. It explodes in her head. She shouts, “NO!”. She sits up brutally, wildly, snatches the scissors, she stabs the hand, she stabs the child, again and again, “enough, die, enough, die, ENOUGH, ENOUGH, DIE!” They grabs her. She loses consciousness, the scissors covered in blood almost engraved between her fingers.

 

Raymond does a last blow of plane on the wooden roof of the day nursery he is building, then decides to stop, quite satisfied : it’s smooth. He tidies up his tools, and walks out of the workshop, he will come back after the nap, this afternoon.

He is eighty years old today, he slowly crosses the small garden between the workshop and the house. He is going to have a glass of wine, in a moment, to go with the meal, a meal without cake or specials. He doesn’t celebrate his birthdays anymore. He is by himself, his wife died years ago, and his daughter left a long time ago to live her own life, away from him. He got used to it, even though, or because, “nothing’s ever fair”, his favorite expression, he uses it every so often, he’s know for it in the village, the old grumpy man.

He gets into his small house, and takes off his work shoes with difficulty, putting them on the stairs going up to the bedrooms, which is nothing but a shoe-shelf now- he sleeps downstairs now, in the old office, he hasn’t been up there for… A long time. Once again, as he does everyday since, like, forty years, he wonders how it looks like, up there, and thinks to himself that he would eventually have to go there to see, one day.

Raymond has no TV, no internet, no friends, no siblings – no siblings anymore. He is alone, and will die here alone, his bones more and more painful tells him better each day. But he doesn’t care. He doesn’t give a damn about the whole earth, as long as he can have his favorite meal, quenelles with sauce he buys ten at a time, and drink a bit of wine on his bloody birthday – and work wood in his workshop, his one and only hobby.

He makes a toast to the Mayor, which ordered him the nursery he is building, and drinks down a glassful of the cheap wine with an unhappy smile.

Someone rings the doorbell. It has been not working for a long time, but there is still power in the button, he can hear the click. The visitor, quickly, starts knocking on the door. Raymond grumbles, he is not expecting anyone, and if it’s another lost tourist looking for the church, he is going to get told off!

He laboriously gets up to open the door, and faces a forty or so couple, ready to insult them, but he doesn’t have enough time to : “Mister Martin? We’re policemen, Sir”, says the woman holding up a card crossed with a red, white and blue strip. “Can we come in?” Raymond is surprised, he is knitting his hairy brows, it can be nothing but a mistake. “Police? Well, you’re a pain in the ass alright. But I guess you can…” He had time to insult them, finally, giggling inside as he lets them in.

 

“What an old dirty asshole!” Simone watches Marc, who just let that out, holding him a cup of coffee. He has shadows under his eyes, she must have too, she smiles at him. “You bet… People like him, you don’t meet them everyday, hopefully.” They’re exhausted. They have been taking turns to question Raymond for twenty hours, and the old man didn’t say a thing, nothing, not a word – well, yes : a big load of insults, enough to write a dictionary, in the end they didn’t even mind anymore.

Simone has been teaming up with Marc for three years, now, at the juvenile police division of the big town near the village where Raymond lives. They have seen everything, heard everything, be it horrible cases or all kind of denials, but this…

“You know what’s pissing me off, Commandant? It’s that I have to send him the doctor every three hours! Damn, I would rather strangle him with my bare hands, with no remorse, with all due respect…” Simone smiles, she is used to the quick temper of her colleague, a little younger than her – and she has to admit, they have a “good one”… “Don’t do this Marc. Don’t make me fetch you a lawyer!” They laugh, jokes about lawyers are their main source of relaxation. “Don’t worry, with the money we earn, I’ll get legal aid.”

They smile, but not for long. Deep down Simone absolutely wants this investigation to succeed; She always wants, for each case they investigate, but in this one, it’s even worse : the old man has to confess. She owes it to Justice. And, above it all, she owes it to Raymond’s daughter. Just thinking about her at this moment, and her eyes start to itch, she, who got back up from everything – at least, she thought…

The arrival of their young colleague Olivier stops her thoughts, and it’s for the best, because, even with the tiredness and this horrible case, she would never cry in front of her men, not even this time. She gives him a questioning look. “Well, guys, sorry, I don’t have much to say. I’ll make it short : the few neighbors who knew him at the time of the case never saw a thing, I only know he wasn’t going out often, only the little girl to go to school and back home. An old lady remembers she was pale and looked sad, and that one day she left with a friend, she doesn’t remember how old she was at the time. And I can’t tell you about all the senile people I had to talk to…” Simone smiles, even though she was hoping a bit more, you always hope for a bit more : “-Serves you damn right, officer. And give a little respect to your elder. Anything else?” “-No, I got squat. The old guy is known in the whole village, but nobody know a thing about him. He doesn’t go out and doesn’t talk to anybody, nobody visits him, the mailman laughed and told me he only brought him electricity bills and his pension, and that he insults him on a regular basis. The Mayor describes him as some kind of local attraction, they have, they have a few visits because of the Romanesque church, see, he curses at tourists asking him for directions, he has a shitty proverb about the world’s fairness… The Mayor told me it’s a brave guy, the oldest of the place, and that he gives him a few woodwork as he knows the guy likes that, such as the nursery which he is building, and that’s all : Nothing report, as you would say in this case, I guess…”

Marc loses his temper again : “Shit! We’re going to find a way, somehow, that’s not possible…” Simone raises her hand, wanting to calm him down : “Don’t worry, I swear we’re not letting him go like that… We have got the daughter’s claim, the result of the search, don’t we? Good, we have to renew his detention.1 You call the proc’, right, and do the paperwork? I’m going to tell Raymond about his Miranda Rights, I feel like you’re not in the mood to do it…”

Marc nods and gets up to call the Attorney Office : “ – ‘K. Spit on him for me.” “– I’ll do so…”.

Simone finishes drinking her coffee, and gets ready to go explain to Raymond they were not done with him, and that his detention was going to be renewed; she doesn’t know it yet, but she will be happy walking back up from the cell, where the old beast waits while preparing his next abuse : while the last time, he didn’t want a lawyer, “what for, I did nothing”, this time, he will ask for one. To her, this means that behind his behavior and confidence, Mister Raymond Martin starts to get a little scared…

 

Xavier is sitting on the couch in the living-room, head between hands, staring into space. He is crying, once again. The phone starts to ring, he jumps, and decides not to pick up the phone anymore, he can’t. It’s inevitably one of their friends, his or her, who is either already aware of the tragedy and would try to comfort him, which nobody can; or doesn’t know it yet, and, worst of all, will happily ask if “Everything went fine”, friendly complaining about not having heard from them, and, for Barbara’s friends, moaning that they can’t reach her through her cellphone…

 

What a stupidity, to have send a message to every people they knew, when Barbara’s water broke and they left to the hospital… They couldn’t know, of course, nobody could. But each call he answered since is a punch at his stomach, and brutally throws him back under the rough light of the delivery room, when, dazed, he saw Barbara… When Barbara, his Barbara, his wife, became… Crazy. Huge tears are dropping from his eyes, he is sobbing heavily, he is under constant conflicting feelings; and horrible pictures, which can’t repeal. Camille, tiny, withered, curled up, covered in blood, dead, just born, Camille, small baby fervently desired, already loved, who left nothing but her first scream to her dad, these first tears that Xavier hears over and over again stopping sharply, cut off. Barbara, always so pretty, hideous, a mask of hate over her face, eyes rolled upward, white, like those of another dead body…

All of his loves, teared apart at the same time. He doesn’t know anything anymore.

He didn’t go to see Barbara. He doesn’t know if he should. Nor if he wants to. He doesn’t know if he still loves her. He bears a grudge against her for what she has done, and more over for what she never told him, if it’s true, for all these years of lie, or more precisely, of silence… Damn, if it’s true, and he doesn’t seriously think it is not, not after he had seen her in this state, not after what she has done, then, how did she keep quiet, how did she think she would heal up by herself..? And what was the value how the love she pretended to have for him?

He gets up, finally, wiping away his tears with his sleeve, because someone is ringing the doorbell, which shakes him out of his morbid thoughts. His legs hurt, he feels like he has been staying prostrate for too much time. His sister, his little sister, was one of the first to call him of course, and to her, he told her what really happened – to others, he only talked about an accident, said the child died, and Barbara was being hospitalized, and he was going to be at her bedside; no, thanks, he didn’t need anything; it would be fine… But to his sister Juliette, he immediately told everything, the truth, and even the atrocities Barbara told to the cop, a few hours later, when she had regained consciousness, tied up on a psychiatric bed. He had to start over fifteen times, for he was crying too much and had trouble talking. Juliette had never seen him disarmed, her big brother, but this time… She only said, in the end : “Stay there. I’m coming.” He halfheartedly protested, she was living all the way across France, but she was a stubborn one, she kept on, before hanging up the phone telling him to go to sleep, she would be here tomorrow.

He opens the door. Juliette backs up a little when she sees him, he has a scary face. But she smiles at him, opening her arms : “Hi, bro…” He falls between her arms, crying louder than ever.

 

Simone sent her teammate home, to have a few hours to rest, after the “ Proc’ ” extended, after Marc’s demand, the detention of Raymond. This means that they have twenty-four hours to get something out of him else than denial mixed with insults.

When she told him what happened and gave him his rights, he asked for the doctor again, of course, she had already predicted that; but also, this time, a lawyer. She took note, without showing she was surprised, and called the lawyers duty office, Raymond didn’t know one, he has asked for a public defender. A young woman picked up the phone, and she explained her briefly the case, extended detention, the man has not seen anyone yet, he didn’t want to at first,  temporary charges : aggravated rape. The voice of the young woman wasn’t friendly, Simone found her pretentious and not very involved, moreover, she didn’t try to talk a bit or know a little more, and the lawyer didn’t even bother to say a polite phrase at the end of the conversation. She smiled unhappily while hanging up the phone, thinking that Miss Princess would have a fun time with Raymond, who Simone would “take back” after the lawyer’s talk.

The Talk is going on right now, the lawyer came quickly – first impressions confirmed, it was a young woman, all stiff in her cleaned clothes, looking uptight, and whose only question, “How long do I have to stay?”, showed how involved in her mission she was, and how much criminal law was important to her… Small meanness to teach her to be polite, Simone told her the interview will have to take place in the cell, the visiting room being occupied, if “Miss Attorney” agreed, Raymond being the only one in there. She agreed, Simone thought she must have never seen, or smelled, any of the disgusting cell of the Republic, where she would get her branded coat dirty, you’ve got to enjoy the little things; and she confirmed her that Law stated the interview should last thirty minutes top, while leading her down to the basement of the police station.

So, Simone is waiting, in her office drowned under case files, that her colleagues come tell her the lawyer left. She takes the papers displayed in front of her, the statement of the old man’s denials, statement of the few witnesses made by Olivier, still hand-written and waiting to be typed later, the statement of the search, unbelievable. The statement of the one and only declaration of Barbara Martin, married name Marcent, terrible, dramatic, unbearable. Barbara, heard under medication, in this white hospital room, a few hours after her colleague from the crime squad had called Simone, to tell her something unbelievable : he was investigating a case of infanticide, a woman just stabbed her just born daughter, in the delivery room, horrific. Good people, married for ten years, forty or so, they had trouble to have a daughter; the husband was shattered, no previous sign, nothing : she just went crazy, and lost consciousness… But he was not calling her about that : the woman, when she woke up, was lucid, well, as one can be, and she immediately proceeded to tell her own story : her mother died when she was very young, she stayed alone with her father, and her father… Her father was raping her ever since, constantly, every day, for years, often very brutally…

Simone interrupted her colleague, frowning : “-But, didn’t you say she was forty years old, and that they are married?” “-Yes, she is thirty-two precisely, he is forty you’re right. But see, and by God I swear, Simone, we’re both old hands, I have never seen something this sad : the woman, Barbara, tells everything in the present, but she talks about her childhood, do you understand? It took us a while to figure it out, we were helped by the doctors by the way, but in fact, she sees herself as a little girl, she came back twenty years in the past in her head, and she tells that stuff as it happened yesterday, you understand? Fuck, I got myself recording the deposition of an incest told by a little girl in the body of a woman who just killed her baby… We couldn’t manage to go all the way through. And frankly, I’m telling you, I cried my eyes out…”

Simone had closed her eyes.

They decided it would be better to split the investigation between the infanticide and the one they had to carry on these old incestuous rape, they were going to ask the Attorney’s Office about it, but he wanted to warn her about it before, out of courtesy for his colleagues, knowing well, and he was almost sorry about it, that was going to be in charge of it – and Simone understood well that by “better”, he meant also : “better for my own mental health”… She thanked him for warning her, and, less than an hour later, she was indeed put in charge of the investigations about Raymond.

She had called the psychiatric hospital, asked if Barbara Marcent’s state was good enough to question her, they got her in contact with the departmental head of psychiatry, and she knew him well by the way, because he was also one of the expert witness she met often in her cases, who told her that yes, she could interview her, but also told her about her condition,  absolute decompensation, wide amnesia, a patient who truly thought she was a little girl, who suffered until now from the brutal sexuality of her father, and who just go out of it using scissors. He also told her that to his mind, delusion provoked the tragedy, and he would deduce abolition of moral discernment for the murder of the child, if he was asked in court about it.

She went to hear Barbara.

She told her how she was living, in an absolute fear, alone, forsaken by everybody, in the hands of her father, who has raped for the first time when the tombstone of her mother’s grave wasn’t even built, she was nine years old. And all the time ever since. She didn’t have the right to play, to go out, except for school, but she would go, come back for lunch, go back to school, and back home, he was checking the time, she couldn’t hang out, no friends. He would beat her, also, sometimes when she didn’t want, sometimes for nothing, or a bad grade. “He beats me with the white cord to plug the bedside lamp. And he threatens me, you see, he shows me scissors, or the tool he has to cut wood, he puts it under my eye and tells me “you shut up, now”. Sometimes he ties me to the wall in my room, there is a hook, it’s dark in my room, I can’t see anything all night long. I don’t tell anything to anybody, or else I would have no house anymore, and he is going to kill me. Say, he is going to kill me, isn’t he?”

Simone, she went all the way through. It was her job, and her fucking duty. She asked this woman the way she has been asking, for years, her usual “children”, her small helpless and crushed victims, she had, as she did for them, tried to reassure her as much as she could, using child words, trying to calm these hands she saw clenching on the bed – she listened to her describing the abomination, from the first to the last minute, of what the woman-child in front of her wanted to talk about, feeling irrepressibly growing inside her an awful rage, white and cold, the absolute will to get the bastard responsible for these horrors – for they already knew, from the quick investigations already carried on, that he was apparently still alive…

She couldn’t ask Barbara about how she managed to get out of this jail, the crushed brain of this woman had stopped at an earlier period, she was still in there, in her torture room, Simone was thinking that she must have stayed in it for years, at least until she was sixteen years old, while school was still compulsory, and that it took a miracle, they would try to clarify this later.

She softly asked Barbara, after three hours which seemed like three years, if she thought she had told everything, by now, if she could leave her have a rest? And the little child told her with her woman’s voice yes, but… “Hey? If you go to my house, can you bring me my wubby Jean-Jean, please? It’s my bunny, I have it all the time, but I think I forgot it, I never forget it, because it’s my friend.” Simone swallowed her tears : “Yes, sweetie, don’t worry. Where is it, in your room? I will bring it to you.”

They went upstairs, during the search, of course – you could see the stairs and the first floor was disused, the old man confirmed he hasn’t been up there for years, everything was covered with a thick layer of dust and spiderweb, the small landing was covered with rats or mice excrement.

To the left, his old room, dirty, a wide bed without bedclothes, an empty closet, nothing else.

To the right, her bedroom. The door was swollen, the wood had moved over time, they had to kick it open. Small room, opaque curtain, which felt in pieces when they opened it, spraying dust all over the place. Sick white walls, floor. A small one person-sized bed, iron posts, moth-eaten mattress, a grey sheet, with near it, directly on the floor, a lamp without a lampshade, plugged in the wall through a white extension cord. In front the door, a toy box, and pieces of what must have been a straw chair, on the left an old closet. Some old toys in the box, some for younger kids, some for teenagers, a few damaged books, textbooks too, up to 9th grade. Empty closet, but on the top of it, with other old things, a dirty fluffy rabbit, missing part of an ear. Sealed in the wall, behind the door if you close it from the inside, a ring. Letters and half-erased drawings under it, on the wall.

Both policemen, Simone and Marc, looked at each other in front of this wall, without a word. Simone took Jean-Jean – she would have bought one if she hadn’t find it, but she was happy it was still here.

Simone takes her eyes off the notes on the search, which had not shown anything else, and looked at her watch, again really impatient to face Raymond. “Come on, Miss, ten minutes more and you give him back to us, right?” Her phone rings, like an echo : “Commandant ? The lawyer is done with your guy, but she wants to see you, concerning her observations.” Simone frowns. “I’m coming.”

 

Juliette, a little because she is a doctor, mainly because she loves her brother, did a lot, in a short time. She let him express himself, of course, she was overwhelmed too, taking for once the part he always played, as her big brother, since their childhood : the one of the protector, the confidant. He calmed down, and decided he had to eat, for he hasn’t eat a thing since the tragedy, she made them something to eat, despite his protestations. They talked, a lot. Then, Juliette finally convinced her brother to take a little sleeping pill, even though he hates any kind of drug, and never slept well; he collapsed, he is sleeping on the couch, no sign of relief on his face, but he is sleeping.

Juliette also called the hospital, using her professional position as well as the fact that she is Barbara’s sister in law to get in touch with the psychiatrist in charge of her, who told her about Barbara’s condition. Juliette hung up very moved; she likes Barbara, they are the same age, and she knows how happy she made her brother. But she didn’t let Xavier see anything, thinking her job was to get him back on his feet, as soon as possible – if it was possible…

The doctor and her agree : the more Barbara will see things that can make her “come back”, the bigger the possibility for her to get out of her nightmare, even though it’s still very early, even if her diagnosis is pessimistic, at least on a short to medium term; now, what’s worrying Juliette, is the ability of Xavier to take this other shock, and, even if she manages to make him go, the ability to see her in this state, without even being possible to do anything about it…

Juliette hesitates, she doesn’t know which decision is the good one, she knows three things : her brother is strong, but he is sunk in an awful tragedy, which she couldn’t have handled, and, from his words, from his behavior, she can tell his pain is unimaginable; she knows by experience, that wait-and-see is not always a good idea, and that the care and attention that, maybe, one would have in more “normal” situation, doesn’t make sens in this kind of situation : he will eventually has to confront it for real, and see Barbara again, even “like that”, he can even blow away some of the brutal pictures that haunt him; and she knows, finally, that she loves him, loves them, both. She is really afraid she is wrong, she doesn’t want to cause him any more pain. She decides to call her husband, a doctor too, facing everyday death and families : being a cardiologist in a hospital forces you to face the incomprehension and pain of the siblings…

Michel’s comforting her, telling her everything is fine with the kids, who sends her kisses, and he quickly understands his wife doesn’t know what to do, and tries to back up in front of an obstacle which, to his mind, seems unavoidable. He tells her what he thinks, frankly : they have to tell Xavier, and take him there, at his wife’s bedside. Juliette explains him that, among other things, Xavier told him about his deep doubts about his love, about wanting to see her, about the horror of the crime he just lived, and the following revelations, which currently make him think he doesn’t know her, that she lied the whole time and is nothing to her eyes… Michel, always a bit harsh, but she loves him for that too, the comforting side of a concrete block in the the tempest, sweeps it all away with one tirade : “Yes, of course, he can’t think else way, not now. But you know them as well as I do, you know him better than I do. He is madly in love with her, it will not go away just like that, I’m sure. And I’m telling you, to go see her in the condition she’s in, will make him understand better than anything else that it wasn’t her, at the hospital, and what kind of secret she was bearing, and how impossible it was for her to tell him before… Your brother, he’s a fighter. What are you suggesting? Waiting until he learns it another way, and feels guilty for not doing anything, and blames you for not telling him? You want to try and put him back on tracks? Give him this fight, right now. If you want, I will call him and explain it to him.” Juliette smiles, tired and anxious, but thankful; Michel just made the speech that, no doubt Xavier would have made if he was him, if it was the other way around… “No, you’re right, I’m going to do it, as soon as he wakes up, and I will try to make him go to see her”. She thanks him and hangs up, promising to call him again soon.

She sits near her brother, he grabs her hand in his sleep, she tighten hers around it.
The lawyer looks upset, she sharply addresses Simone as soon as she sees her, at the other end of the hallway. “Ah ! There you are. Well, I saw Mister Martin, he told me everything you’re blaming on him, and moreover, when it took place! That’s absolutely crazy, according to him, what he is charged with is twenty-five years old, do you confirm that?”, she squeaks with an outraged look.

Simone looks at her, a little dazed, ready to tell her to watch her tone, but something holds her back. “Hu… Yes, Miss, that’s about it, around this time…”. The lawyer carries on : “But..? The prescription time limit has expired.2 You’re holding this man for an event that can’t be prosecuted, it’s too old, it’s expired! That’s why I asked to see you, to tell you I’m leaving notes, and will warn one of my fellow at the Courthouse as soon as I get there : it’s expired, and I explained it to my client, I’ve shown him the statute law, he can’t be charged, it’s over, ten years after his daughter turned eighteen, that would be twenty-eight years, it’s over since then, you’ve got to release him, and he is eighty years old!” Simone blinks, stunned, she hesitates for a split second, then : “Oh, I see, it’s expired! Oh, too bad, we didn’t see it at all, us, down here… Crap, well, we will see what we are going to do with that – anyway, thank you a lot, right, we won’t have to work for nothing : thanks a lot… Well, then goodbye, Miss, see you soon, I hope.”, she answers, turning back, without even bothering to know whether the young lawyer will understand the sarcasm – she thinks she won’t, she doesn’t have a clue, truly, she is already handing over her notes to the officer at the front desk, and she would have told her how wrong she was, if she had been polite and humble, but in this case, she can just work it by herself, Simone hopes she will be ashamed a little later.

To say, she could have told her that, even if she wasn’t making a mistake, even if the delay had actually expired, she would still have kept him, and try to get a confession, legally useless, but fundamental, at least to her mind, for the mental health of an hospitalized woman who just committed and unbelievable act… What for?

She goes back up to her office, in which Marc is waiting her, he came back in the meantime. “Hello, hard worker. You are not going to believe me, but you know what the old man’s lawyer just told me? And I thought I had seen it all…”

Of course, they had, immediately, asked themselves about the possible time limit, alongside with the Attorney by the way, like they always do when the events are very old. And in Barbara’s case, precisely, they had thanked the God of Police that the tragedy took place this year, and not the following, for Raymond would have automatically won, and this thought was unbearable.

The poor Barbara was thirty-two, and she had denounced, or rather the little girl trapped inside her for all those years, acts of rape committed on herself since she was nine, by her legitimate father, and probably kept on for the major part of her childhood.

Then, contrary to what the lawyer squeaked, none of these events had hit the time limit : article 7 of the Criminal Procedure Code, even as bad-written as it is after several modifications and additions, left no doubt : twenty years and only from the time the victim is major (the age of majority is eighteen years old under french law), in this case, until she was thirty-eight. And then they went through all the statute law modifications over time that Simone kept up to date, to check that in the meantime, no older and shorter prescription has been acquired3 , but there was none : no prescription, Raymond was could lawfully be prosecuted, and sentenced.

The policemen didn’t understand how the lawyer made such a big mistake, and talk about ten years instead of twenty, the twenty years time limit has come to effect for quite long time by now (and if they had known that Codes available at the duty office, where she got one, for she wasn’t used to criminal law and didn’t have one, were more than six years old, they would probably have understood better…), but the fact was that Raymond, apparently, must believed it, now – especially if the lawyer had been as peremptory as she was in front of Simone.

Marc opens his mouth first, after they both laughed about lawyers full of themselves, who sometimes should refrain themselves : “Say, Commandant, I know you’re not going to like it, but…” Simone stops him with a hand gesture, there are some things you don’t say out loud. “Yes, I know, I’ve thought about it, you know. I’ve been doing nothing but thinking about it…” Simone is fundamentally honest, it’s one of the reason she wanted to be a cop, she believes, simply, that everyone should be too. But, between day to day work, in appalling conditions and over constantly awful cases, and the big theories, she already had to find, sometimes, an in-between; to find a red line not too straight, not too curved, and it’s sometimes complicated. She knows exactly what Mars is thinking : since Raymond believes he is out of trouble, and will stay unpunished, to use his mistake, in order, and according to what he showed of his delightful personality, filled with nastiness, it could work, to make him talk, to make him tell, to make him confess, with no fear of going to jail, for “it expired”…

It means, even if it works, he might not want to say something anyway, of course,  that Simone should not tell him about his mistake, or tell him the real rule, even, and she knows it,encourage him to keep mistaking. Well, it’s not her mistake, it’s not like she was getting ready to torture Raymond by beating his skull with a phone book… No, of course, but it wouldn’t be “fair” either, and Simone doesn’t like that… He is not, “fair”, Raymond, that’s the least you can say, about this bastard… But Simone knows this last point is not a valid one, and that starting to think like that, may lead to various abuse, since there are always bad guys on the other side – it is taking the risk to end up acting like them…

She temporarily settles the argument : “We will take him back, for the full detention if we need to. We’re going to get him. And if he doesn’t confess, we will think about the damn prescription, before the end, OK?” Marc is about to protest, he would rather crucify the old man right now; but he sees the look in Simone’s eyes, the determination painted on her face at this moment, and just nods. “Let’s go”.

 

Juliette didn’t go into Barbara’s room, she thought it was better for Xavier to go alone. The psychiatrist too, but he got two nurses ready, they’re here, on the other side of the door, in case Barbara has another fit, nobody knows what seeing Xavier will do to her, after all she stabbed his hand first…

Juliette is not a believer, Xavier neither. But she is praying.

 

The temporizing Simone wanted was useless, she should have guessed that Raymond would immediately put his foot in it, more over, this guy is as brutal and straight forward as a raging bear, he shouted at them as soon as he sat in the office : “So, the dynamic duo, good one, looks like your old stuff can’t make a trial, too late, “perscription”?  Can you tell me, god dammit, what are we waiting for, and what am I still doing here?”

Marc reacts instantly, faking a half-sad, half-angry look, talking to Simone : “See? I told you she would warn him… And there it is : he will not say anything, now…” Simone looks at him, really angry, but she doesn’t have time to reply, Raymond talks again loudly : “You bet! I wouldn’t have said anything anyway, wanker, for there is nothing to say! So, you let me out or what? It’s not really nice at home, but it’s less bad than here…”

Simone breathes in, and makes him sit back : “Calm down, Mister Martin. We’re not doing anything for now, you sit back, and we talk. Let me explain it to you, we, don’t decide whether you’re charged or not, you see? I’ve already explained it to you, we listen to you, we take notes, and we give them to the procureur, and he will make the decision, alright? And the procureur, you can go see him quickly, or in twenty hours, it’s up to you; because us, don’t really care about Law, what we want is the story, the facts. The truth. We don’t mind working for nothing, it’s our job, so we will keep you as long as we have to, until we have it, the truth, in two hours or in ten, it doesn’t care. Then, the procureur, he will apply the Law, and good for you if you’re not prosecuted, it’s none of our business. So, let’s go, shall we start all over again ?”

Simone is a little red, she rose her voice, but not too much, she stayed calm, Raymond sat back, she can see he is thinking, she can almost hear it, Raymond’s eyes are looking at the desk, where legal codes are standing, up-to-date ones. She looks at Marc, standing by the door, and slightly blinks with both eyes, a small gesture toward the exit. Marc gets it, risks their all, and storm out : “Fuck, I can’t stand it. I’m out of here.” He walks out, slamming the door.

Simone hopes she has face of the policeman ready to take his time, neutral look, eyebrows slightly up, ready to start the question all over, both hands over the keyboard. Twenty seconds later, Raymond’s look didn’t leave the desk, but she can see his mouth widening on one side; she sees he is about to pull out a grinning smile. She’s waiting for him.

 

Barbara didn’t recognize Xavier. Not at all. She asked him, with the voice of the woman he loves, but the tone of the little girl she hid, who he is, what he is doing here, if they were going to eat soon, and why he is crying.

A brain wave happened, but in Xavier’s head. He wanted to yell : “Why am I crying Barbara? Because I love you. Because you loved me. Because we were made for each other. Because my life is meaningless without you. Because, even now, even after all of this, you’re beautiful, like you were when I met you. Because I need you, because you have to come back, because I’m all by myself, dammit, because you’re my wife. YOU ARE MY WIFE, what are we going to do, without you, what, what do we have left..?”

But instead, he gets up, grabbing the bed’s posts, holding back his tears, stopping thinking about him, eying intently the only woman in the world who shall never disappear. He fakes a smile, and with the same tone Barbara is using, he tries to reassure her, pretends to be a friend, who she is going to see often, for she had an accident and is going to be healed, she is going to recover, he will be there, he will come back, all the time, keeping her company and helping her recover – but no, he is not crying, or only because he is happy to see her : she knows it, Barbara, that sometimes, people cry because they’re so happy, so moved..?

He got out of the room for the little girl he loves starts blinking, she sleeps a lot, she takes pill for that. She told him she was glad to have a friend.

He slowly closes the door, Juliette is here, he has a pale smile, he joins her on the bench, closes his eyes with his head between his hands. “She didn’t recognize me. Nothing happened. She is stuck there, she talks to me like a ten years old girl. But I have… I’m her friend, she… Liked me. She wants me to come back.” He sits up straight, looks at his sister, with fiery eyes, the eyes Juliette knows, those of her strong big brother : “Tell Michel he is a dirty bastard brother in law. But not this time : damn, I swear, little sister, I swear… I will bring her back.”

It’s little Juliette’s turn, this time, to have shining eyes. He takes her in his arms, holds her tight, and whispers “thank you” in her ear.

 

Raymond looks up, and indeed, he is smiling, half-smiling, some kind of two balls glowing of trickery inside his eye-sockets. “Well, after all, right, my commandant, it pisses me off to tell you, but you’re right, the faster I tell you, the faster it’s over, if it’s better like that, and I can get out of here. So yes, I agree, she told the truth : I had sex with my daughter.”

Simone feels her heart beating again, slowly : “Yes, it will be simpler… Well. But that’s too short anyway, we will start all over again, OK? Try to tell me everything at once, we will not come back on it…”

– “Yeah I’m going to tell you. For all I care. Then, uh… There. In fact, my wife died, the girl must have been, eight or nine years old…”

It took three hours. For this interview, there will be many others. And the old Raymond told everything, everything. In great detail.  Simone agrees by the way that he, immediately, stopped with the abuse and the arrogant behavior, and that sometimes, he even let some emotion out, she didn’t have to ask him much questions, or interrupt him : it was flowing out. She knows this well, often seen among stronger denier, and she has seen a lot of them… Once the lock’s key is found, or sometimes, the lock broken open, they let go, they tell, for deep inside, often really deep inside, it was a burden, weight down by fear, suffered for years, and it’s a relief, in the end, to finally confess…

His initial loneliness, the little girl there, the first abuses, the punishments and threats so that nothing is heard outside the house, the full acts, the girl gradually becoming his wife, the physical punishments, fearing she would tell on him, persuading her that if she says a word, she will go to Hell, the threats, the scissors, the ring, the darkness…  Growing bolder, always more, for he knows she will not speak, she will never speak. He said everything, openly and frankly. Roughly. As he is.

And, one day, he doesn’t know how, the girl must have been sixteen or seventeen, as Simone has guessed, and, simply, she didn’t come back from school, “period”. He never heard of her anymore, and, after a few time, fearing the police would come, he didn’t try to hear from her, fear of suspicion, fear of being questioned. He told to the school and the few people she knew that she dropped out and left with a boy, they were surprised, how was it possible, she didn’t knew or hang out with anybody; but nobody looked closer, in the village, closed door, hidden life. Nothing happened to him, and he kept on, blocked up the stairs, alone, forgetting bit by bit…

Simone looks at him, now, finally having the right to be tired, with no anger, feeling nothing more, like often, this horrible sensation of an awful waste, this void of some lives, and the daily tragedies happening there, the absolute lack of moral in this father, and, nevertheless, the unavoidable fact that he was part of mankind…

Raymond didn’t have a word for Barbara, nothing. We’ll see later, maybe, but she doubted he felt anything… There was other loophole to fill in, many, and mainly how this startled seventeen years old teenager could manage, without help, alone, to get out, and become a “normal” looking woman, claiming to her future husband to be an orphan, and never tell him the truth…

She suddenly feels the urge to hear news about her. Raymond signed, she asks Marc, who came back in the meantime to bring coffee, to bring Raymond back to his cell, and report the confession to the Attorney Office.

Marc nods in agreement, but asks her, as she is walking away to call the hospital, if she allows him, “off stage”, to explain Mister Martin a legal point, just like that, showing him a few terms of the law… Simone looks at him, joyless : “As you want…”.

Raymond is grumbling, “I thought it was over”; Marc confirms with a little smile, “in a moment, yes, I can tell you, it will be over”, he says, leafing through the Criminal Procedure Code. He finds the article he was looking for, and looks back up : “So, Raymond, I heard you keep saying “Nothing’s ever fair”, don’t you?”

 

Around two years later

 

Raymond Martin is sentenced to nineteen years of imprisonment. Heavy sentence, the maximum was twenty years, but the harm was awfully heavy. In fact, his lawyer feared he would get the maximum, which was recommended, according, also, his behavior during the trial – he let out a mouthful of injure when the sentence was read. The lawyer still got one year less, to his mind because of the age of his client, and, also, he believes, because at least he confessed. Raymond yells he is going to appeal, the lawyer sighs…

 

Xavier took it, helped by close siblings, his sister first, friends… For two years, he visited Barbara everyday, and spent all his free-time with her; he kept working only because he needed the money, for her and for him, you can’t live on the air you breath in a hospital room.

He’s just a man, he almost broke down, often, he was sometimes desperate, so little progress, none, in fact, or so little, two or three false expectations from a sentence they thought belonged to the real world, but nothing else… So much unrequited love, living in an illusion with her…

More over, he understood a lot of things, since, by taking the place of Barbara in her father’s trial, by being the claimant in her name, and going all the way for her, discovering the lead weight covering the shattered childhood of the one he loved…

He had to leave her alone for three days, for the first time, because of the trial, after which, still completely crushed after facing all this time the man responsible for all this suffering, Xavier wanted nothing but to rush to her, to reassure her.

But Xavier is just a man, and now, again, it’s too much. Then, once again standing in Barbara’s room, at her bedside, while she is sleeping, he risks it, like he has done before, and talk to her about the real world, stopping for a moment to be only her best friend and pretending to be a kid : without turning the lights on, he opens his heart, he tells her what he went through, he cries, telling his wife how much he understands, how much he knows she couldn’t tell him, how much she wasn’t responsible for anything, never, except for his lost happiness… How much he loves her, and will always love her.

He’s sobbing, trying to silence down his weepings, head down, barely seeing Barbara’s body in the darkness…

And every walls in the world collapse at the same time.

In front of him, the head of Barbara straightens up, and she asks : “Xav’ ? Where are we? And what the hell are you doing in the dark?”

 

[To my little sister in the real life, whose name happens to be Juliette too – Juju, because it has been a long time since I’ve told you how much I love you !]

  1. (Under French Law, a detainee can be held for 24 hours before charge, the detention can last up to 48 hours if authorized by the Procureur, who is equivalent to the District Attorney for the USA, or Crown Prosecution Service in the UK [↩]
  2. Under French law, prescription is an institution which prevents from prosecuting after a given time limit. For instance, all “délit”, which roughly corresponds to felony, must be prosecuted within a 3 years time limit; after 3 years, the prescription is acquired, meaning the case can’t be prosecuted anymore [↩]
  3. When a new time limit is put in use, such as in this case twenty years for crime since the majority of the victim, while it was ten years, until march 2004, this new time limit applies only to events that were not expired by the time they are prosecuted, obviously. For instance, if Barbara had already been over twenty-eight (majority plus ten) in the early 2004, the events committed would have expired by the time the new time limit came to effect, then it wouldn’t have been applied. Barbara was actually twenty-six by this time, the new time limit, in her case, replaced the old one in 2004, for the investigation takes place, let’s say, in 2009. [↩]

REMERCIEMÔS
Ce blog formidable fonctionne sous WordPress. Mô est hébergé par OVH (qui ne demande pas de pub mais quand ça marche il faut le dire). J'y ai usé plusieurs développeurs, qui m'ont aimé puis haï, comme dans la vraie vie... (La plus patiente (l'infobulle qui suit est d'elle...) m'avait bâti un WordPress sur mesure - Tikoun créateur de sites Internet.) J'ai dû le moderniser et ai opté pour le beau thème Fluida Plus, des très bons de chez Cryout, que j'ai totalement massacré puis remodelé, seul et dans la douleur. Les dessins d'avocats en noir et blanc partout sont de la très gentille et très créatrice Oph.
