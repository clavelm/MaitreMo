A ride through Civil Law.

Par Marie Machinerie Le 10 janvier 2012, à 17 h 32. Lu 47 705 fois. Le 15 janvier 2012  audience civile, avocat, tribunal d'instance 110 commentaires

There are not only dark, sad and terrible stories on this blog1 . Of course, there are some, and I don’t wan to “spoil” you, but something makes me think that my next post, if I write it before 2013, will not make you smile anybody. But today, it’s time for a little harmless post, in the “Useful tips” category that Maitre Mô may create sometimes between two autographs sessions2 .

Despite being clearly oriented toward criminal cases, I think that this blog is not read by many sentenced criminals, but probably more read by people who were once involved in a civil case. I don’t want to tell you about litigation in family law cases, which is supposedly the matter that most of you had to deal with, but which is absolutely not my field – it’s the only position I never took in civil law, I think. Today, my idea is to put an end to a months long silence and to the constant reminders of Maitre Mô (“Hey, you’re not writing anything, are you?”) show you a bit of what path you, or rather your case, will follow if you are summoned in front of a court for a “classic”  civil case3 .

If the case you’re involved in has to be judged by a TGI4 , by one or several judges, can be quickly explained : in brief, after a period of time needed to set up the file and documents, which can last a few months, and during it, parties involved will exchange their views and arguments on the case (being represented by a lawyer is mandatory, and the procedure is written), you will be given a date for the pleading hearing, which you can choose to attend or not, most of the files are only given and the pleas, well to put it in a nutshell, they are… useless, since it’s a written procedure.

If you go there, your lawyer will start with something like “I PLEA TODAY IN FRONT OF THIS COURT my client being present AND I WILL BE BRIEF” : meaning that, the client is here and would not be pleased to see his lawyer just giving the file or pleading the case of his life in 1’28, and that he has the intention of talking for forty-five minutes at least. You will then see the judge (or judges) settle comfortably in their chairs, and wait for things to end. You will then see them sag a bit more every time the lawyer would say “I would like to end with”5 . Don’t be surprised if they don’t write down anything that your brilliant lawyer will say : they can only take into account the arguments written in the file. After a long, boring moment for you, especially because courtrooms acoustics are rarely designed for a good sound diffusion toward the audience, the Court will give you a date for the judgment between, let’s say, six weeks and two months later, and you will receive the decision after this time.

You will be really more involved, if you want to, if your case has to be judged in front of the Tribunal d’Instance6 , a court which is different in the way that the the procedure is oral and that you can plea for yourself, since being represented by a lawyer is not mandatory.

Everybody can end up one day in front of a TI, even people who try to respect the classic rules of society and not annoy other people. Even if you’re not a landlord or a tenant, even if you never subscribed a consumer credit7 , even if you ride a bike so that you don’t give money to car-sellers and mechanics, you are always at he mercy of a nice regular of the TI : the litigious neighbor. Most often, the neighbor complains about the water dripping from your gutter onto his wall, HIS fence being often crossed by YOUR kangaroos, or your trees being too close to his property. Thanks to the litigious neighbor, hours and hours of legal adventures are awaiting you. Without the litigious neighbor, judges of the TI would languish between two cases of personal credit and eviction. Blessed be the litigious neighbor who helps reimbursing the state debt with 35 euros tax stamps.8 .

So you’ve been summoned in front of your TI by this litigious neighbor who decided that the shed you built on your several hectares-wide plot of land and several hundred meters away from both of your houses included a rooftop and a gutter which were unsightly9 and that you should be forced to take it down/change it and pay damages for the harm caused by this hideous structure. You have two choices : consult a lawyer before the trial’s date, and send him do his job, or go yourself, with the documents justifying that your shed is perfectly common and harmless (for eyesight), to explain your arguments in front of the judge.

Let’s consider the hypothesis where you chose to not be helped by a lawyer, and let’s go to the hearing in front of the TI. You arrive, a few minutes before the given hour, in a courtroom where back seats are taken by quiet plaintiffs and defendants, and front rows by really loud lawyers. A bell ring, the judge come in, along with a clerk of the court, and announce that he will now call the cases : it means, that he will call each case scheduled for the day, check if the case is ready to be judged, and say what he is going to do with it during the session.

He can also decide to declare the summons void (if the plaintiff didn’t show up at the hearing, and the defendant has no claim of his own), to cancel the case (the parties didn’t do something they were asked to do during the last hearing : giving evidences or arguments, mostly), to withdraw the case from the schedule (parties have asked the judge to withdraw the case, often because a transaction is being negotiated), to postpone the case to another hearing after being asked to do so by one of the party if it is justified (or if judges are manifesting against a recent inappropriate presidential speech, in this case all the cases will be postponed), or to hold the case, which means that it will be heard after all cases have been called.

When your case is called, you get up, announce that your case is ready to be heard, and, if your malicious neighbor agrees, you will be told that your case is hold and will be heard later.

A hearing in front of the TI usually involves from 50 to 100 cases scheduled, calling the cases may take a while.

Since you are waiting, you may entertain yourself by trying, for instance, to identify the member of each of the following categories of lawyer :

– the lazy : you will hear say about more than four different cases that “submissions are under review from the client” (secret language often meaning “submissions have not been reviewed by my own brain, it would have been hard for me to give them to my opponent”). Often noticeable from the number of cases which are canceled during the same hearing by the judge, more and more abruptly each time.

– the talkative : ten minutes argument to justify/oppose each postponement demand.

– the absent-minded : he forgot its schedule (the Court fax the schedule of the hearing to the Bar Association so that it is then sent to the lawyers), forgot that one of his case was scheduled to be heard during today’s hearing, says that he will give his file later to the Clerk office (because he forgot it), goes back to his office even though he still has one case left, comes back at the very end of the session (called by a sympathetic fellow lawyer) and realizes he forgot his robe.

– the lawyer of institutional tenants : half of his opponents are not present, he gives each of his files for the cases where they are present while buzzing something which sounds like “bzzz bzzz agree on the delay bzzz bzzz clause of forfeiture in case of non payment bzzz bzzz”. Often looks depressed.

– the almost retired : reads, more or less furtively, the daily paper during the call of the cases, hides more or less skilfully his cup of coffee under the table, is poked with elbow each time one of his case is called, and content himself with telling for each case that “my partner will conclude”, “my colleague received new evidences” or “I will tell my partner about this cancellation”.

– the lawyer who often defends credit company : looking resigned for, helping the “bad guys”, he loses quite often in his cases in front of a Court who has the power duty to pick out irregularities10 generally in favor of the borrower. The judge has not even finished telling all the irregularities, that he has already written them down. He spends his time telling to borrowers, who don’t understand lawyer-judges dialogue : “Don’t worry about it, he is asking me for elements, that can’t be bad for you.”

– the criminal law specialist : the never-failing clue to recognize him when he comes or leaves the courtroom is the robe, always rolled into a ball under his arm or crammed into the bottom of an already full of cases bag. You can also spot his painful and surprised look, like a hen who just laid a cubic egg, when the judge points out that there seems to be a “clause of forfeiture for the right to interest coming from the lack of respect to the annual duty of information toward the borrower” in his case of consumer’s loan : if there is one thing that the criminal specialist doesn’t like, is to stand in for his partner specialist of civil law in a hearing, while he has a preliminary hearing in a criminal case in fifteen minutes and it’s been a long time since he last worked on a loan’s contract, leave him alone once and for all.

– the taxation law specialist : warning, VERY dangerous species for your concentration. Noticeable because of his troubles finding his way in the courthouse and his very clean and well ironed robe, which looks new even though he is well in his forties : in 18 years, it’s only the third time he comes to the TI for a case, since his specialization is mostly about giving advice and not pleading. Most of his fellow lawyers think he is an outside lawyer (from another bar association). He will later speak for 40 minutes to explain painstakingly the mechanism of collecting Value Added Tax and the sale of real estate from a farming partnership to an Unincorporated Business Trust, and his opponent, a tax law specialist too, will do the same. At this point, look at the sad and panicked look of the judge who realizes that in order to start to understand what it is about, he will have to spend at least a day looking through the statute law on financial regulations, and he is not even sure to find all the document in his courthouse.

– the barn : no, it’s not nice to call them like that. But how else do you call a group of five to six female lawyers who systematically sit in the front row to loudly talk about their kids/their annoying clients/ their divorcing colleagues/their fellow lawyers becoming partners/ the Dukan diet/ the size of their colleagues butt/ the facelift of the president of the Bar/ who’s that one – what bar is he from – no wait, I think he is a tax law specialist of our Bar?

Once all cases have been called, the judge will let the lawyers speech in the order they chose, with some exceptions (the defendant who came with five loud kids who couldn’t be looked after by someone else, to answer to her tenant is a good exception). Generally, the order is : lawyers from another Bar (lawyers from Paris’ Bar are the easiest to spot, since their robe is “virgin”, meaning that the usual epitoga11 doesn’t have fur), the current President of the Bar, former presidents of the Bar, and then other lawyer according to their seniority (not long ago, I saw a lawyer who took the oath as a lawyer in 1956 and pleaded before everyone, even Presidents of the Bar, other lawyer clearly fearing that he died before the end of the hearing) then, finally, people appearing in person.

While you are waiting for your turn, lawyers’ speeches will give you the occasion to notice other lawyers categories : the flatterer (“it will not be left unnoticed to the court great wisdom whose wise decisions are known and respected” – I might overdo it a little, but not so much), the lawyer who has an empty file (doesn’t talk about his evidences, use a lot of convoluted synonyms of “you will make your opinion considering the given evidences” or some sentences he took from the flatterer), the one with a verbal tick, usually “indeed”, and you will easily see that some of the other lawyers12 are counting the number of times the tic happens, with the help of the “little sticks” technique in order to evaluate his performance : they do the same for each of his speeches13 .

Then will come the time for you to explain yourself about the case of the gutter with the litigious neighbor : you will both come forward, the neighbor will say every bad things he thinks about your shed’s gutter and ask for 5000 euros of damages for the moral harm, and you will let him talk as much as he wants (at least, until the judge tells him that he is well enough informed about the situation) or nobody will understand a thing. You will then speak, and give, if available, a certified report (or else, pictures) proving that your building is not that unaesthetic, built at a reasonable distance from your neighbor eyesight and that the harm is not proved. You will let the judge make you neighbor be quiet when he will try to interrupt you (the best behavior for you, to my mind : stop speaking with a weary look until he tells you to resume, showing that contrary to you you neighbor is a badly brought up boor). If it is the case, you will make a claim for damages for improper proceedings, for you have other things to do of your time rather than defend the aesthetic design of your gutter against your litigious neighbor.

If you had any special expenses (like transportation to go to the courthouse) during this procedure, you will not forget to give supporting evidences of them to the judge who will then be able ask your opponent to give to you the amount of money corresponding on the ground of the article 700 of the Civil Procedure Code – if you win the case.

And of course you will leave all of your evidences and written arguments, if you have any, to the Court when you will be asked, after your speech, to give your file. The Court will carefully14 , along with the decision. It seems obvious, but I have already seen an old man telling that no, it took him long enough to set up his file, that if the Court wanted one, they just had to do it themselves, and went away with his two files of documents under his arm.

Then, the judge, that normally disappeared behind a wall of cases files, will give you the date when the decision will be given, between four and six weeks later if the Court works well, or a few months if it doesn’t15 , and will tell you that you will receive the decision directly at home, in the days following this date. You will leave the courtroom and avoid, at best, to start a fight with your neighbor in hallway16 , and will receive six weeks later a decision from the judge who will consider that your gutter is perfectly in accordance with what a gutter is commonly expected to look like and that no harm was suffered by your neighbor. There.

This will end your marvelous trip through the country of TI, at least until the day comes when your neighbor will ask for the demarcation of your lands with shared expenses (ouch). Or until one of your child will try to put you under guardianship, or if he can’t decide whether it is best for you to be incinerated or buried in the family vault. The TI has a really wide jurisdiction…

A last precision, nonetheless17 : being alone as a defendant or a plaintiff in front of the TI is your choice, but it is risky, and asking another professional whose job is not to represent and defend you can be dangerous.

This way, a plaintiff thought it was better (and cheaper) to ask the bailiff who was going to deliver the summons to also write it, for a case against a car mechanic. I got a summons :

– quoting Code articles regarding liability for a breach of a contract (relevant) AND a breach of a duty under negligence (irrelevant in the case), which is a legal non-sense, although it’s not something that would stop a judge of the TI;

– stating arguments regarding both liabilities;

– following an emergency proceeding, aiming to give to the plaintiff a judgment for a provisional refund, which is only possible when the existence of the duty can’t be seriously challenged;

– in front of the TI, while due to the amount of money demanded, the trial should have taken place in front of the Tribunal de proximité18 ;

The lawyer of the car mechanic came to the hearing and pointed out that the TI had no jurisdiction over the case to follow an emergency proceeding, because the existence of the duty was seriously challenged, and even for a classic procedure (the amount of the damages demanded were around 2500€).

The poor plaintiff, who might have won her case, found herself lost since she thought, as she said during the hearing, that such a petty case didn’t need to be handled by a lawyer whose fees would have been almost as high as the damages she asked for. Instead of saving money, she had to pay the bailiff expenses who wrote the summons and the costs of the trial, without the failing reparations being repaid.

To my mind, I would advise you, except for a really simple case where you would only have to defend yourself, to look for the help of a lawyer19 . Each man his job.
