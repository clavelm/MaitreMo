Accueil Merci d'être venu ! “All rise !”

“All rise !”

Par Maître Mô Merci d'être venu ! Le 23 mars 2012, à 15 h 37. Lu 52 282 fois. Le 3 avril 2012  Assises, crime, feu, président, témoins 48 commentaires

[ This story – sometimes tough, warning, it’s the story of a criminal trial – was written by T0rv4ld (for the users of the pompously called “social network” Twitter, just kidding, it’s awesome, quick introduction ), famously “unknown”, not a lawyer at all, who had the smart curiosity to go into  a Cour d’Assises1 , and the generous idea to suggest me to publish the result, drawn from the notes he took during a two days long trial. I would like to thank him very much, for at least three reasons :

1/ For a lot of good reasons, even though I don’t sleep much, I can’t manage to finish one of my text right now, for instance I had a funny one for the 200th post of this blog, it will have to wait a little more, and so will fifty or so works in progress…

2/ It will be the 200th of this blog, and, as it owes a lot to its readers, it’s not that bad that it was written by one of its reader – and also that I received that gift since it’s the birthday of the blog, four years old a few days ago.

3/ This text is really interesting, I think, for it shows what criminal justice really is, when you take some time to watch it work (Go on!), even with not trained eyes : it’s the greatest Court in France – even if know it works with six jurors instead of nine, amputated from three thinking units…

If you’re not a legal professional, it will make you want to see it by yourself; and, if you are a a professional it will make you realize what stands out or doesn’t, for the “audience” – and God knows, that it is important, isn’t it dear colleagues?..  And, it will remind everybody that a trial in front of the Cours d’Assises is mainly what the presiding judge do with it…

Thanks again to the author, I will now let you sit next to him in the courtroom…

This text is in two parts, the second one will be published in a few days, in about a week (It’s not my fault, it’s him !). The black footnotes are from the author, mine are written in blue, couldn’t hold it, but the author gave me the green light.]

[From T0rv4ld : in respect of the victim, the defendant and their relatives, this story is anonymous. You should know however that it was a public trial, anybody could have come and see it.]

 

Two weeks before the trial

 

Opening the newspaper randomly during breakfast, I read in a small articles that the trial for a case that made quite some noise two years ago will be held at the Cours d’Assises of my region. The plot? A man was accused of spilling gas on his wife before setting her on fire. Something that can make you sick… I look at the date of the hearing, I’m free for the two days when the trial is supposed to take place. I have been reading the intense stories of Maitre Mô, and the procedural crusades of Maitre Eolas2 , it’s time to push the door of the courthouse and see what’s behind it.

 

First half-day

 

It’s 9:30 am, I am sitting in the second row, just behind the victim’s relatives.

A man in a gown3 , look at us, then shout :

“ALL RISE !”

Everybody stand up at once ! I hurry up and do the same, dropping half of my stuff doing so, and finally look up to see the famous Court coming in. A man with a red and black gown, who is the presiding judge, comes first, followed by two women wearing a court dress. The first judge sits in the middle of the bench, a woman sitting on each of his sides, whose role I don’t know yet. A third woman comes in then, and sits on the far left side, she is also wearing a gown. Finally, an older man sits on the far right; according to his look and his small round glasses,  I bet he is the Clerk of the Court ! It is confirmed a minute later by the presiding judge. On the other hand, first disappointment : we don’t know who are all these people : lawyers? Judges? Magistrates?

Now that the Court is ready, the jury must be set up. The judge announces that the juror will be randomly chosen, six of them since January 1st 2012. In fact, seven will be chosen (6+1) among thirty pre-selected people, as the trial will last two days, the presiding judge wants to give himself some leeway in case one of them doesn’t make it into the second day. Clever. The defendant’s lawyer have the right to reject four of them, and the prosecution, represented by the District Attorney4 , three. The presiding judge take this occasion to remind the jurors that they can’t, of course, be related to the case or the relatives of the victim or the defendant. He tells them their rights and duties, mainly regarding their duty to keep the deliberations secret and their duty to be neutral. I can’t resist to tell you the speech of the judge, here it is (yes, it is really only one sentence, a long one that law-enthusiasts will love) : “You swear and promise to examine with the most scrupulous attention the charges which will be brought against X ….; to betray neither the interests of the accused nor those of society which accuses him, nor those of the victim; to refrain from communicating with anyone until after your finding; to heed neither hatred nor malice, nor fear nor affection; to remember that the accused is presumed innocent and that he has the benefit of the doubt; to decide according to the charges and defense arguments following your conscience and your innermost conviction, with the impartiality and resolution that befit a free man of integrity, and to preserve the secrecy of deliberations, even after the end of your service”5 .

As a proof of good faith, and one after the other, they will have to face the judges and say “I swear it!”, with their hand in the air. A quite funny story happened during that ceremony : one juror swore with his left hand, causing indignant mutterings among the crowd. Yet, it’s true that it is authorize, simply because not everybody is right-handed. Flawless logic6 . They will all finally take place one the bench around the three judges, in front of the audience.

The presiding judge then asks the bailiff, “the man-who-announces-the-court”, to  name the witnesses who will be summoned in front of the court during the two following days. Those who are present are invited to leave the room immediately, and will have to come back when they will be summoned. This in order to not be influenced by other witnesses7 .

The presiding judge read the charges and gives a quick summary of the facts and the first conclusions of the experts. He gives the sentence that the defendant might be condemned to (in this case, criminal imprisonment for life). He also gives the other sentences the defendant has been condemned to, for assault. Finally, the presiding judge reminds that the defendant is presumed innocent.

The trial is interrupted for fifteen minutes.

When the trial resume, and after the now famous “ALL RISE !”, the victim takes place on his bench, on the left side, surrounded with some relatives (brothers, sisters and parents) and the defendant takes place on a chair in a glass dock, on the right side. He came into the room with his hands behind his back, so the presiding judge immediately asks the two policemen around him if the handcuffs were taken off (which is the case); one shall appear free in a courtroom, until justice decides otherwise.

The presiding judge then starts speaking, and start a dialogue with the defendant in order to understand his story and know him better. He will ask him to speak about :

– his childhood, between school, where he went until he was 14 and didn’t like, and working in the fields, which he did every evenings and week-ends;

– his family, large, humble and from the countryside, including seven brothers and sisters with whom he has little ties (none of them has visited him in jail for two years and a half…);

– his professional career at several wine growers after he got his CAP89 , with who he had some troubles (threats, verbal and with some tools…);

– his hobbies, well, mostly hunting, fishing and pétanque10 .

The judge also notices that the defendant has already been twice in rehab for alcoholism.11 .

The parties having no question, the hearing of the witness is about to start; there will be eight of them (five to talk about the time before the facts, three for what happened after…), and psychological and psychiatric experts. It must be known that only a few of the people who were interrogated during the investigations will be summoned in front of the court; a dozen of people, from his school teacher to his CAP’s colleagues, along with the bartender of the local pub were asked for the need of the investigations, but the ones summoned are only those who are the most important; for the others, their statement will only be read.

The first witness is his former partner. After swearing to “tell the whole truth, nothing but the truth”, she is free to say whatever she wants to.12 . She will talk about marital abuse and death threats she got regularly from her ex-partner. We also learn that they had a daughter, that the defendant never acknowledged13 , and that she kept seeing him even though they were not together anymore – “He would sometimes kiss me and I didn’t push him away”. Yet, some parts of her story will appear to be confused, for the simple and good reason, as the presiding judge will notice with a quite ironic question (“Do you have a memory problem or are you scared?”14 , that they are very different from what she said to the investigators after the event took place… The judge notes that the former partner has an economic link to this case, for the house where the defendant and the victim lived is still half hers, and the defendant was giving her a monthly pension. The district attorney requests the right to speak and asks a few complementary questions to the witness in order to try and clarify her statement.

The second witness is a neighbor. He indicates that the defendant was sometime violent toward him, and he often took in his ex-wife and daughter, when the defendant scared them under the influence of alcohol. He also points out he was scared for his own daughter, who he didn’t let near the defendant…

The last person to appear this morning is a psychiatric expert. He quickly restates the biography of the defendant and shows the result of the test he performed on him : the subject has really low intellectual capacity15 . Fire, which is designated as the modus operandi, is described as a “classic” mean among rude subject on the countryside, especially as a symbol of destruction. The defendant is uninhibited under the influence of alcohol, and slightly paranoiac. However, he has no mental trouble; an important detail, since if he had one, this would have made him not liable and had an important effect on the upcoming judgment. Finally, the  psychiatrist underlines the possible link to the fact the defendant’s father lost a lung, in the past, while he was taking care of a fire as a fireman.

The judge comes back on the alcohol issue, and asks him if a new rehabilitation is possible : the answer is yes, but only if it is voluntarily chosen, so that the rehabilitation is chosen, and not forced, so that it can be efficient on the long term. But the defendant doesn’t want to.

The trial is suspended, adjournment (it’s already past 1:00 pm). It will resume at 3:00 pm.

 

Second half-day

 

When the trial resume, a third witness appear, as friendly and nice as a rugby player after a post-game party. So much that he will be lectured by the judge for mistaking the Courthouse with the bar that he seems to visit often. It’s a good friend and a neighbor of the defendant. He points out the jealousy of the defendant toward him, and tells the story of the boudin16 , which will come back often, like a running gag, until the end of the trial. To sum it up : the day before the event, the defendant asked this neighbor to come around 4:00 pm to make some boudin together. This neighbor having a little business on his own, couldn’t make it on time and arrived late (after calling to warn him), making the defendant angry and giving him a reason to drink way too much, so much he crashed his car into a street lamp! Anyway, when the neighbor arrives, the defendant is laying on the floor, dead drunk. The victim and the neighbor decides to make boudin together anyway, with the help of another friend (witness number four, coming next). The making taking some time, it’s late when the defendant wakes up, and he starts to insult his neighbors, accusing them of “pounding” his wife. And, contrary to the statement of the ex-wife, this witness knows exactly what he is saying and has no hesitation nor inaccuracy. He adds that it happened once that the defendant threatened him with a hunting rifle, before finally shooting at the ceiling !

Then, witness number four, working as an accountant and friend of the couple, confirm the statement of the previous witness word for word (even though he didn’t see the previous statement, I remind you). He says he left the house because of the threats of the defendant after the boudin’s episode.

From now on, and until the end of the day, the people who will come to make a statement came into the story after the facts that the defendant is accused of.

The first one is a fireman, who came after a phone call of the victim herself, around 6:30 pm.

He finds her almost naked, with only a towel around her hips. He notices right away that she is severely burnt. The health of the victim became worse, according to him, once she was laying down, and she is driven immediately to the closest hospital. The judge asks a question, rightfully : why didn’t they use the helicopter ? The fireman answers that, given the distance and the time needed for landing and take-off, it would have been the same. The fireman also states that he saw a man in the other end of the yard, hiding in the shadow, looking threatening17 .

The second witness of that kind is a civilian, who found the defendant sleeping in his car, after the event. He is the farmer who owns the field where the defendant was found after a search by the policemen. His statement will not give many new pieces of information.

The third witness is a medical examiner, who examined the victim once she was transported at the hospital. His conclusions are stupefying : 69% percent of the body’s surface is burnt, 33% is third-degree burnt (highest possible) and 36% second-degree burnt (highest and lower parts). In short, only the head and the shoulders of the victim are not burnt.  The treatments give after the event were quite heavy, and they are still undergone today, more than two years later – some wounds fore instance are still not completely cured… A lot of skin grafts, from the scalp, were made. During rehabilitation, the victim broke her thighbone, causing many walking troubles. Finally, the list of complications given by the doctor are simply astonishing : low tolerance to sun exposition, many allergies, bad cold/hot feeling, inability to do most kind of sports, walking troubles, high risk of infections especially if she has to be hospitalized again…

It’s in that kind of moment that people in the audience look at each other and think to themselves that their little everyday problems are absurdly insignificant compared to what this woman had to suffer and is still suffering, days after days…

In the end, photos will be give to the jury and the judges, who will turn pale when looking at them – some jurors, despite their obligation to not show any emotions, looked like they were about to be sick18 …

The hearing is suspended for fifteen minutes – some time to let the jury get back on his feet…

Last part of this day (it’s already 6:00pm), with one of the most awaited expert : the fire expert. Awaited for, with an exemplary scientific rigor, he will refute absolutely every versions of the defendant, which were :

– that sandals soaked with gas and a cigarette butt started the fire;

– that a puddle of gas, used to clean up a stain on the floor (…), followed by the fall of a cigarette paper, caused the victim to get on fire.

Yet, the examination of the clothes of the victim (classified as evidences), of the residue burnt and not burnt, and the reconstruction, prove that to his mind, “beyond reasonable doubt”, that the gas spill was made in the back of the victim, and toward the ground, causing a pool to appear on the ground, and the fire was then set with the use of a burning sheet of paper…

Even the argument of the defendant’s lawyer, being that the back of the hands of the defendant were lightly burnt when he was found in the field, is explained by the “flash” caused when starting the fire – and not by the help he said he tried to give to his wife (in this case, the inside of his hands would have been burnt, not the back).

According to his observations, the expert evaluates the amount of gas poured to two liters. Miss the District Attorney takes this occasion to speak, and come back on the different versions given by the defendant, underlining the fact that none of them was possible, according to the expert’s opinion.

The next to last witness is the OPJ19 . He will be brief, but underlines that the defendant has no consistency when speaking, really hard to understand (indeed, each time the judge interrogated him, he spoke really fast, without articulating, making each dialogue difficult). He indicates that the versions given during the procedure changed greatly, ruining his statements’ credibility.

Finally, to end this (long) first day, an investigator from the police comes in. He restates the facts which happened the day before the event, and how they caught him, through a man hunt in a cornfield in the middle of the night (!). He talks about “premeditation” (for the first time in the trial), based on the witness’ statement dealing with death threat on his partner.

He then asks the judge if he can put a new evidence in the file : the recording of the phone call made to the emergency services. The judge accepts, and with the agreement of the victim, decides to play it in the courtroom20 . I’m not going to lie to you, it was awful. Try to imagine the statement of a person, burnt from head to toes, who has to struggle to get to her phone, dial the emergency number, wait for someone to pick up the phone and then states clearly and in a clear way her first name, last name and address, while she is suffering greatly, and I think you will start to imagine what the victim had to go through that night. More over, she had to say everything twice, once to a fireman, then to a doctor, who you can imagine was stunned : “What do you mean you’re burnt everywhere??”…

In the room, not many people didn’t crack up that evening, the victim and her family first…

It’s time for me to do a first summary of this day : emotions, cruelty and incomprehension toward the case. You can start to wonder how a human being can end up doing such things…

On the legal side, the “ceremonial” side can be surprising, at first, and is even sometimes funny, for instance as when the presiding judge told one of the witness, who didn’t greet him the way he was supposed to, by saying “Your honor”21 : “It’s not a question of the judge’s ego, it’s a question of principle and custom. We are between adults, inside a Court of Law, not between high school’s friends”. A presiding judge who by the way, is an excellent speaker, easily understandable and able to ask the right question at the right moment. I still don’t know what is the role of the two women around him, who never talk : are they assistant? Judges? Will they take part in the deliberations? And I still wonder what exactly is the role of the District Attorney?

I go home with these questions, and many others. I might have answers tomorrow, when I will come back to see the second and last day of the hearing…

REMERCIEMÔS
Ce blog formidable fonctionne sous WordPress. Mô est hébergé par OVH (qui ne demande pas de pub mais quand ça marche il faut le dire). J'y ai usé plusieurs développeurs, qui m'ont aimé puis haï, comme dans la vraie vie... (La plus patiente (l'infobulle qui suit est d'elle...) m'avait bâti un WordPress sur mesure - Tikoun créateur de sites Internet.) J'ai dû le moderniser et ai opté pour le beau thème Fluida Plus, des très bons de chez Cryout, que j'ai totalement massacré puis remodelé, seul et dans la douleur. Les dessins d'avocats en noir et blanc partout sont de la très gentille et très créatrice Oph.
