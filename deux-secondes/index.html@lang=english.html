Two seconds…

Par Maître Mô Histoires vraies Le 10 mai 2011, à 6 h 29. Lu 40 441 fois. Le 10 mai 2011  cassation, légitime défense, responsabilité, violences 139 commentaires

This nightclub has a bad reputation, even though Omar doesn’t know that: right in the middle of the old quarter of Lille, close to the Courthouse, in some kind of basement under a dilapidated building,  there is not a week without a fight happening there or some kind of accident  taking place just in front of it, when it closes and the few remaining drunkard leave.

But Omar, thirty-three, accountant, wedded, who never set foot in there and never drinks, has to meet a friend this night, who he hasn’t seen for a long time, who works in a nearby restaurant, and arrived from the same African country as him this week : it was agreed that if he had not finished his shift at midnight this Friday night, Omar would wait him at the bar of the nearby nightclub.

That’s why he is there, in the crowded basement, leaning on the counter – not aware that his wait will not last a few minutes, but will eventually bring him into legal, Kafkaesque, six years long journey…

So Omar is here, at peace, drinking a glass of orange juice, when it happened : in the crowd, there are three young men, obviously drunk, dressed as women, and whose main activity, following the Holy Mission they gave themselves, which is to celebrate the Bachelor’s party of the taller one of them, who is getting married the day after, as they keep yelling it, consists in throwing the soon-to-be husband on other people dancing, laughing out loud like idiots when he hit someone, proving that alcohol always help modern mankind to find new intelligent things to do, as everybody may know…1

Omar, there for twenty minutes, watched that for a moment, from the corner of his eyes, and noticed that people reacted either by smiling and gently pushing away the husband-to-be, or not smiling and pushing him away in a stronger way – in both cases, the man is laughing, his blond wig crooked, his make-up running from the heat, and the rolling gait of a man about to throw up and, Omar thought to himself, whose wedding day would be a bit tough…

He doesn’t think about it anymore and turned himself to the bar when he is hit in the back by the man, just as he was about to take a sip of his orange juice, the shock spilling some on his shirt; he turns around, facing the man, who stinks of alcohol and has a stupid smile on his face, and he pushes him away with one hand, maybe a little coldly, since he goes flying on his two friends, and the three of them fall, limply, on the ground – Omar is an accountant, but he is also athletic and well-built, and they were already unsteady anyway.

Omar is asking the bartender for water, to try to clean his shirt when the taller one, the soon to be wedded, who got up first, furious, in his back, comes back to grab him by the shoulder, forcing him to turn around, and spit in his face, yelling, not smiling anymore, that “you’re not the one to throw me to the ground”, and headbutts him. Omar takes it, his forehead splits open, and, as the man is coming toward him again, this time with his arm raising, clearly not to pat him gently, he has two reactions : he takes off his glasses with his left hand and throw them on the counter, and at the same time swing his right fist into the face of the man, forgetting he is still holding his glass : it shatters, and the man falls back, yelling.

The bartender grabs Omar, who doesn’t resist, the other guy has blood all over his face and is lying on the ground, with his friends, the crowd scatters around, people call the police and the emergency services, people are running and yelling everywhere; Omar calmly told the bartender he wasn’t going to leave and would wait, the man released him, and he is sorry, really, but he doesn’t feel guilty, not at all; he wants to kneel down near the husband-to-be, whose wedding is definitely going to be quite bad, but his friends doesn’t let him do so, with a mean look, so he waits.

Two guys come to him, and give him a card with a phone number on it, if he ever needs their testimony, they saw everything; Omar thank them, but tells them he probably wouldn’t need it, for he just reacted after the violent attack of the man; both men agree, but one of them tell him to keep the card anyway, just in case.

The police and the emergency medical service arrive at the same time, and find Omar standing in front of the bar, a cut on his forehead, some people around him, two tough security guards between them, the three young men unsteady, two men helping a third one to keep standing up, all three dressed as women – the man got up since, his face covered in blood, fakes hairs stuck on his face, and insulting Omar, in such a way that nobody can understand what he is saying.

Is it because he is standing up, and that it all looks at this time like a small fight, or is it carelessness, or the tiredness – it is one o’clock in the morning, and it’s not the first intervention of the night, like every Friday..? Anyway, police will not hold anybody, and will not take any witness phone number, except for the two friends of the bloodstained man – one of them is his future brother in law by the way.

They ask them to come the next day to make a statement at the police station, and let them go with the injured man and the medical staff, to Lille’s hospital.

In a few words, the bartender explained the situation, which is quite clear anyway : for an unknown reason, he didn’t see the beginning, Omar and the other man fought, the man had bumped into Omar, who had pushed him away, he came back and Omar hit him right in the face, with his glass – he didn’t see the headbutt, he was busy searching for a napkin to help the man clean his shirt.

Omar, after a small time at the hospital with the policemen, in order to stitch up his small wound on his forehead, will be put into custody right after, and will be heard – of course not, he will not ask for a lawyer, what for?

He will call his wife, she then will warn his employer that he would not be at work, at least in the morning, and he will tell the same story, exactly as it is written above, without changing the least bit of it.

He will always try to be as precise as possible, for during the interrogation, he will learn at the same time as the policemen, that the case is finally a bit more serious, actually : the hospital told the policemen the wounded man, François, had an blood alcohol content of three gram per liter of blood; but more over, they were trying to save his left eye, which was injured by a fragment of glass…

Quite more worried now, and really startled at the huge stupidity of all of this, Omar will take care of describing precisely the event three times in a row, and especially, answering questions, the exact way he “committed” the act.

“He was walking toward me, threatening, after he headbutted me; I didn’t think about it, to protect myself. I took off my glasses, I was afraid he was going to hit me again, and, in the same time, I threw my arm in his direction, I wanted to push him away. You’re asking me why I hit him with my glass : I’m telling you I wasn’t even aware I was still holding it…

– Q : You are saying that you took off your glasses at the same time you violently hit him : these two movements doesn’t look consistent. Are you sure you didn’t plan on hitting him, and took off your glasses to protect them in case of a fight?

–  No, it all happened at the same time, I took off my glasses with my left hand, and hit right away. I didn’t think about it.”

The bartender confirmed that François, then completely drunk, had already bothered several clients, the policemen were understanding, for Omar had no criminal record, whereas François had two previous small convictions for battery, during the last four years.

Things got more complicated the morning after, when the two only “witnesses” came to the police station, the bother-in-law, and the best friend of the “victim”, still a bit drunk : they both agreed they drank a lot, that’s the whole point of a bachelor’s party, and bothered “not meaning harm” several persons in the club, they also both agreed that François, thrown by them, bumped into Omar, but none of them, unfortunately, saw the beginning of the next scene (they were both lying, dishonest enough to help a friend, not enough to lie completely and pretend there was no provocation : the easy way out was to not have seen the beginning…), they just noticed their friend was coming back toward Omar, probably to apologize (yes, they were in fact quite dishonest), when Omar grabbed his glass and violently hit him…

The wound on Omar’s forehead? They didn’t know, probably happened in the rush…

The policemen went to the hospital to hear François, still drunk, his face bandaged, and waiting for a surgical operation, and he confirmed word for word this version : no headbutt, the rush, he got up and tried to go talk to the man, and he didn’t have time to say a word when he was hit in the face… Of course, he will file a complaint : he was supposed to get married that day, and instead, he was said he could lose an eye…

Omar could do nothing but explain everything all over again, to much less nice officers now… He suddenly remembered the card the two other witnesses gave him : he explained how he got it, and that he didn’t know them, and that it was taken during the police search he had.

The policeman called the number, a cellphone, but the police statement, just four lines, stated that the man was working, and couldn’t come at the moment, but could testify on the behavior of François, and the end of the event, but he didn’t see the victim hitting…

The victim, still in the emergency room, could not be heard during the time of custody2 , and could not be confronted with Omar : in the following afternoon, the Parquet3 ordered the policemen to keep investigating, waiting for further information about his health, and to let Omar go, they would summon him later if needed.

A policeman told him, very nicely, to do so, so he did : before leaving, Omar filed a complaint against François for battery.

Things were like that, and Omar, getting back to his day-to-day life, and he did not hear about this for two years : he was never summoned, and didn’t have any information about his own complaint, but he didn’t really care, for he had filed it just in case, like the policeman told him; nor about François’ complaint, sure he was that they thought he only defended himself, and was not being prosecuted.

It’s a letter coming from the CPAM4 of Lille which would prove him wrong, quite abruptly, and to say, make him completely panic : the Organism, in this mail, was asking him to pay as soon as possible thirty-four thousand euros, amount he was sentenced to pay by the Tribunal Correctionnel5 of Lille, and in the same decision he was found guilty of the harm caused to François…

Now finally, he understood, but a bit late, that things were getting out of hands, Omar decided to see a lawyer, who he told that he indeed changed his address six months earlier, without thinking about telling the Parquet about his change, as ninety-nine percent of average people waiting for potential prosecution, which may take several months to show up…

I immediately understood he was really wrong, but also hoped immediately that the crucial decision was made “par défaut”.

 

[Criminal Procedure time6 ]

The aforementioned mail stated the date of the decision, and the first reaction, nervously, of the said lawyer, a really nice guy, was to immediately check on the way the trial was made, before checking its content : the decision could be made in three different ways concerning Omar : adversarial hearing (in french : contradictoire) (but he had to be there to do so, and he was not), adversarial hearing subject to notification (contradictoire à signifier) (he would have been considered lawfully summoned, but decided not to come at the hearing: the allotted time to make an appeal starts in this case only when the decision was notified to him by the Parquet, and this notification was not made yet according to Omar, who didn’t get anything, then we could still appeal the decision), or finally by default (par défaut) (the Court judging him but admitting that he was not lawfully summoned, which is an excuse for his absence : in this last case, the decision must be notified, but when it is notified, the defendant has another possibility : the opposition, which is a way to nullify the decision and be heard once again by the Court, for a real adversarial hearing this time, which then can be appealed – since he wasn’t summoned the first time, he still has the right to appeal the decision).

[End of the Criminal Procedure time]7

 

In his case, I found out, fortunately, that it was a hearing by default, which could be set aside with an opposition, opposition that we made the exact day we first met : Omar could be tried once again by the Court, this time being there with his arguments.

Although… As always in a bad case, this one was going to, you will see, never stop getting worse, and to start with, it got into a bad procedural twist, which we couldn’t avoid…

You remember that Omar also, in a preventive and cautious way, filed a complaint against François? Well, the Court had linked both cases, meaning it considered that it was the same case, and that it could be heard together and judged in the same decision; and, after this link-up, sentenced Omar, for what he did to François, but also, in a consistent reflexion, acquitted François for what he supposedly did to Omar, the victim in this case.

You would say, for you often talk in a bad way : “Hu yeah, of course, logical and so what?” And I would answer, that it is, indeed, logical, but I would also answer that the decision, rendered toward Omar was made by default, but the decision made toward François, was adversarial, for he was at the trial!

This also means, I will keep it simple here but it will be one of the major point of the hearing later, that if Omar could indeed make an opposition to set aside the part of the decision that found him guilty and sentenced him, this opposition could not be made against the other part of the decision were François was acquitted, this part could only be appealed – and even then : Omar was not part of this trial the first time, of course, he wasn’t able to join the procedure…

So, to put it in a nutshell, at this point, the decision sentencing Omar was not definitive, and was even nullified by now, but the decision acquitting François was final, and couldn’t be reversed anymore…

I guess you didn’t understand anything of the last four paragraphs : I confirm that you are average human beings, who are not concerned by these procedural rules; remember at least that nothing had stopped the Court to say in the same decision, that Omar as a defendant had a good reason not to be here and to have never been aware of the date of the hearing, but at the same time to forbid him definitively and for the same reason to be heard about his complaint – it should not be possible, but it is.

So, it wasn’t great, but not too bad in the end – and in any way it would not stop us, of course, to argue that he was compelled by self-defense (french : légitime défense).

Don’t think it is easy to prove this : Case law defines and applies strict conditions (immediacy of the defense, proportionate means of defense, absolute necessity of the defense…); but, on one hand, I am an optimistic man, it’s even my job; and on another hand, I was immediately convinced that Omar was telling the truth, I found him nice and calm, during the interview, with his wife, both, clearly, sorry about the consequences of his acts,and not only for themselves… It was obvious that there was no previous sign that could show he was about to get into a fight with a stranger that night, no alcohol, no violent behavior and no criminal record…

However, my experience, which I hate sometimes, and I miss the first years where I had no memory of bitter failures to refrain my spirit : I know that a victim covered in blood is often right, more than anybody, in front of any court – fair or unfair, it doesn’t matter, it is a human behavior, and the judges, most of them8 , are human beings : if you put a healthy, clean man standing up near another man, damaged, bloodstained and down on his knees, anybody would rather be sympathetic to the injured one.

And in this case, François was the one to be pitied, even if his behavior that night was stupid : he definitively lost one of his eyes…

According to this fact, acts of violence defined by the Penal Code being more strongly punished when their consequences are heavier, for it is a crime proportionate to its results, Omar had been prosecuted for acts of violence causing mutilation or permanent disability, and could be sentenced to up to ten years of imprisonment, in theory…9

But anyway, there was, really, a lot of things to work on, and we were going to fight. I wrote a letter to the CPAM telling it to keep its note aside, refraining myself to write more precisely where I thought it would be best to put it, since I was making an opposition against the decision, and waited, confident after all, for the date of the new hearing.

The new judgment had to be different, not only because it would be really adversarial this time, not only because it was afire, not only because Omar was not looking for trouble and only had a reflex, not meaning harm… But also because in addition to the thirty-four thousand euros that the CPAM obtained, a total of almost one hundred thousand euros was obtained by François as damages…

Obviously, since Omar and his wife earned together around 2.500€ a month, with two children and a new house to pay off, it was impossible for them to pay such damages…

Regarding this, however, another nice procedural complication weighed on the scale (which started, I should have thought, to be in really in favor of the same person, but the fact that I was on the “good” side made me forget to that kind of details…) : as he had the right to do so after the sentencing of Omar, François’ Lawyer immediately filed an application to the CIVI10 in order to obtain the payment of the damages, which were paid by the Fonds de Garantie11 .

Nothing serious either – but the modification of the decision, and, as I was hoping, the acquittance of Omar, on the criminal and civil action, at least reducing his liability, would imply not only to suppress of reduce the amount of the damages obtained by François, but at the same time forcing him to pay back, all or part of these damages : not easy, I guess, for a judge – who should, as we should, not take into account these information, of course, but who would actually, as we would, notice them nonetheless, I bet…

 

A year after the first decision, precisely, we were back in front of the same Court – and when I say the same, I am not talking about the place, but there were the three exact same judges.

I can tell you I did my best, I believed in it, really – truth is, I still believe : the first aggressor was in fact François, Omar only defended himself, his responses was only self-defense, bad luck he was holding a glass at the moment he hit, but without being aware of it, and we can even wonder what would have happened if he had not stopped the second attack, even though he didn’t want to do it this way… The main point, as it will always be, was whether the response, using a glass as a “weapon”, was or was not proportionate to the first attack, which was made bare hands – I kept telling that to my mind Omar was never aware of using a weapon, in the legal meaning…

 

A year and two hours precisely after the first trial, the Court made a new decision, on adversarial hearing this time, exactly similar, word for word, to the last one, on the issue of the liability and the damages – only the punishment was changed, it was at first one year of suspended imprisonment, and the new sentence was three months suspended, I should have been glad, but I didn’t really care, and so did Omar…

I was, to tell the truth, so sure that we were going to win, that I had only brought two written statement of the two witnesses who gave their information to Omar, who had called them in the meantime : these statements were swept aside, and I was wrong.

 

We appealed the decision – and I promised myself this time I was going to summon in front of the Court the two men, who Omar didn’t know, and whose dispassion could not be challenged…

As I said earlier, when a case starts bad, it usually goes the same way as it does in real life : you can try whatever you want to save your boat from sinking, and even, with some efforts, get it close to the coast… It doesn’t change the fact that you are actually sinking…

The hearing which took place in front of the Court of Appeal, two years after the first decision, was really rough, and, to my mind, quite obnoxious, as much for the way my witnesses were treated, clearly suspected of connivance, and for the way they treated my client, and myself – for instance, they were asked in front of me if we had ever met or talked, right in the beginning! And I instantly lost my nerves, for, as many nice people, when someone has the uncanny idea of challenging my honesty12 , especially when I am working, and more over in the middle of a public hearing, I feel, as I felt it the few times someone did so, what is known by pilots as “Red out” : all the blood, either because of sudden movement of the plane, or because of the “G” handled by the pilot, goes up to the head and blinds, and may cause said person to faint.

Well, same thing happens to me, but only for the blinding part, because I don’t faint, it only upsets me, whoever made the stinky supposition, be it a judge (not the presiding judge in this case, harsh but courteous) – since I am sure that if somebody is ready to suspect a lawyer of seriously illegal comportment, and making up witnesses is one of them, this same person is also able to do it himself, and therefore deserve no respect anymore.

Anyway, lots of mutual yelling, incidents during the hearing, and even an interruption, and so on… Nice atmosphere in front of the Court, where I pleaded for more than an hour, which is already a sacrilege, but more over I was yelling, sickened…

I knew when I left, even though I didn’t know the decision yet, that I was wrong, even if couldn’t have done anything else; I told Omar I was sorry, who told me he understood, given the tone of the hearing, and at least he saw my involvement in his case – I’m telling he is a good guy… But I still felt guilty.

And it got worse when I got the decision : confirmation of the reject of self-defense, and confirmation of the guiltiness; increase of the punishment “the sentence given by the first judges being not proportionate to the seriousness of the case”, it was back to one year of imprisonment, fortunately still suspended, and, icing on my cake, confirmation of the civil decision, rejecting my proposition of shared responsibility between the defendant and the plaintiff : the Court, in an opposite way and for good measure, considering that “the response being not proportionate to the attack, and therefore, self-defense and shared responsibility could not be used”, decided that Omar was fully liable and “had to compensate the risk of hurting badly the man in front of him” – enjoy this last sentence, it’s the kind of sentence that comes right into your face, when you wonder if anybody, at any moment, tried to go down into this basement, and imagine himself, even for a split second, in the shoes of Omar, drinking his glass and getting headbutted…

The court denied the self-defense, considering that what I pleaded, the fact that Omar was not aware of holding his glass in his hand when he hit the man, was impossible, more over because at the same time he took of his glasses, an evidence or at least a sign of reflexion : I believe now, frankly and with hindsight, that it is something that can be discussed – but at the time, I didn’t think so.

When you read this, in your quiet office, the day of the judgment (you didn’t go to the Courthouse, but asked a colleague to take it down and fax it to you), you’re hurt, and you’re angry; but also, almost every time, you feel guilty for what happened – even though it is clearly not your fault, but in this case, you know very well that you had upset the judges…

And after this last confrontation with yourself, follow immediately another ordeal : you call your client.

 

The allotted time for an appeal in front of the Court of Cassation is really short, only five days after the decision was made, so you have to quickly stop whining, try to come to term with the failure, immediately, and make a decision – you don’t make an appeal in front of the Court of Cassation without thinking about it, for it involves high expenses.

In this case, however, and I must confess I believe the Criminal Chamber13 would not accept to say if there was or was not self-defense, for it was a matter of facts, which are not reviewed by the Court of Cassation14 . Omar didn’t really have a choice, he couldn’t afford to have a criminal record and to have to pay the CPAM and the Insurance Fund (which had paid the damages, but would later ask him to reimburse them) the equivalent of the price of his house, for years : we agreed that we had to try.

So I filed the appeal, and appointed my usual and excellent lawyer as our representative in front of the Court of Cassation15 , and we worked together several months to set up our claim.16

I won’t go into the details of the appeal, but there were two main points, a criminal one – self-defense – and a civil one – shared responsibility.

 

Three and half years after the first decision, we received the decision of the Criminal Chamber, and we lost definitely on the criminal side, the Court considering that “given the observation made by the Court of Appeal, which show that there was a lack of proportion between the attack and the means of defense used, the Court of Appeal made a lawful decision” : end of the argument on this issue, that we had to accept, even though frankly, I will never totally agree.

We won on the Civil issue: “On the second ground of cassation […], considering than any judgment or decision must include enough grounds to justify the decision; the insufficiency of grounds or conflicting grounds is equivalent to the lack of grounds; that, in order to find the defendant liable for the damage suffered by the plaintiff, the decision states that the lack of proportion between the attack and the defense stands in the way of the share of responsibility claimed by the defendant; doing so, without verifying that the plaintiff did not commit a civil wrong which also caused the damage, the Court of Appeal didn’t have enough grounds to make its decision; the Appeal must then be upheld”.

It was a relief, a bitter relief though.

The Court of Cassation set aside the decision, but only for the Civil part, and remitted for reconsideration the case in front of the same Court of Appeal, but with different judges.

We were now waiting for a new hearing, for which I pleaded only on the Civil matters, pleading for a share of responsibility – angrily, and why not, I asked for only twenty percent of the total amount to be charged on Omar – with the difficulty that self-defense was rejected, but the share of responsibility I was hoping for came from the fact that Omar only defended himself, or at least attacked after he was attacked at first…

It’s important to notice that, since the procedure in front of the Court of Appeal, we have not heard about François, who wasn’t a party of the procedure in front of the Court of Cassation, and would not be a party in front of the second Court of Appeal neither : the length of the procedure doesn’t wear out only the defendant’s lawyers…

 

Five years after the first decision, the Court, after several adjournments since the plaintiff didn’t show up at the hearing, and could not be found, gave its definite decision on the issue of shared responsibility, and gave each party half of it.

We didn’t file another appeal in front of the Court of Cassation against this decision, for we thought it would be hard to have a better one, and this time the Court of Cassation will only confirm the evaluation made by the Court of Appeal : the trial, this small classic trial for some unfortunate and ordinary events, which happened seven years earlier, was over.

Omar went back to his day-to-day life, and started to pay back the CPAM for the half he was still sentenced to, month after month, then the Insurance Fund will ask for payback later, for fifty thousand euros – as his wife told me, in the end of our last interview : “It’s only money, we will work it out. But, it’s just no fair…”

François had to keep on living his life (we learned at one time that he finally got married anyway), with only one eye, and, likely having bitter regret about the stupidity of what happened that night, and also likely bearing a huge grudge on Omar…

Both of them will never be the same again, and the Justice came by, through a long journey, with the only task of judging, in a given context, exactly two seconds, only two seconds, of the life of a man : Omar is slightly bleeding from his forehead and is a bit stunned, surprised too, and François comes back toward him, his fist in the air, Omar swings his arm and breaks his glass on his face.

Criminal Law is often about that, in the end, doing what is strictly forbidden for an historian : judge and rewrite the history afterward.

Was he aware that he was holding the glass, did he voluntarily hit the man with the glass, how much time makes the difference between a reflex, and a reflexion, why didn’t he just leave, or dodge, what was the proportion of control in his reaction…? Of cowardice? Of pride?

I wanted to tell you this story because it reminds how long and complicated the legal fight can be, and how sometimes you need to, just, hold on…

But also, of course, to ask you the question, as I asked myself since of course, about these two seconds, if they were two seconds of your life…

What would you have done?
